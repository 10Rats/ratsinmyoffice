Introduction of Randy

Randy has been committing cannibalism for quite some time. He decides to attend a therapy session as an attempt to recover from his addiction. --Official Synopsis


Characters

Randy (played by Isaac Stonewood) is a cannibal who has stayed under the town's radar for years. He is pretty much your average Joe Shmoe: he works at a typical job and lives in a typical home. He decides to attend therapy with Dr. Cunningham under the guise that he has an alcohol addiction.

Cunningham (played by Wayne Thomson) is Randy's therapist. He leans towards the "I need someone to talk with" therapist role, so he sometimes takes the liberty to engage in a deeper conversation with his clients. Towards the end of the last episode, Cunningham discovers that Randy is the Cannibal Killer.

Altman (played by Mia Dixon) is the detective who recently caught interest in the CK murders after discovering half-eaten remains in a forest. She has worked on a fair share of murder cases before, but the CK case has proven to be a tough one to crack. Her character would have been more fleshed out as she get closer to narrowing down her list of suspects.

Ethan (played by Christopher Walsh) is a close friend/coworker of Randy. Despite the two being friends, their friendship seems... hollow? If you were to look at their conversations, it seems as though a brick wall is talking to another brick wall.

Summary

We are told that Randy has been murdering people for a while. He admits that his addiction is "fucking him up" and make him "feel like shit," so he decides to look for a therapist. Cunningham's name catches his eye.

Some time passes, the cops find half-eaten remains out in the wild. Det. Altman is prepared to crack open the case. Randy is also prepared to throw off their trail should they get too close to him.

Both Altman and Randy makes some progress. A glimpse of Randy is caught on a wildcam footage. Unfortunately for her, she cannot make out his face. Randy finally feels like he is one step closer to recovery.

After a session with his new client, Cunningham suspects that Randy may be hiding a sinister secret. Even though it is not his job to dig too deep, Cunningham could not help but throw some bait at Randy. He succeed in his plan a little too well. Unfortunately, we never find out what happens to the two as the show was canceled at its first season.

History

Randy the Cannibal, created by Shawn McCormick, first aired on Midnight Channel in 1994. This show only aired during late-night, so only a handful of people have watched the show.

Once MC officially announced that RTC's second season was canceled, people gathered on the web to discuss its cancellation, along with the petition to revive Randy. Unfortunately, as I am writing this (2/12/2000), Midnight Channel has no plans to continue RTC.

While the petition was ongoing, people made dozens of fan pages, fanfics, and factions to show their adoration of the short-lived series. Few years later, without any new RTC content, the fanbase slowly dwindled in population. Many pages became defunct and countless fan-made contents vanished into thin air.

I made this website in hopes that I could preserve what is left of this fandom, even though I probably recovered only a fraction of it. I highly doubt Randy will come back (for a multitude of reasons), so I do not expect a sudden surge in population.
